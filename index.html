Our Story


As a self-taught software engineer, I know the struggle that comes with balancing a job, or life, while trying to learn a new skill. Before I even made the career switch to software engineering, I was teaching minorities how to code. I taught people of all ages, from six year olds to sixty and up. Since I don't have an engineering background, the only way I could even teach myself was to understand it in a way that made sense to me, so I could explain well to others. The biggest struggle I had while teaching myself to code, and that I see other self-learners have, is not having a mentor they can checkin with, that can also help them when they're stuck. That's the biggest advantage of going to a coding bootcamp, having teachers at the ready to answer your questions.
